### The Setup

**Data Sources Available to Simulation:**
- 23andMe samples (affluent, very online, self-selected demographic)
- Hospital records at the time (2025-ish)
- Random old human DNA samples scattered around
- Public fossil / DNA records

**Key Point:** "This specific company that consolidated a few bio data firms by buying them up has all the diversity in a few data sets. This is basically whatever DNA samples we have today in 2025 (whatever sources)."

**The Limitation:** "This is some sample of humanity that is insufficient for true population re-creation, but they can somehow back mutate and solve for finding the variations that did occur in the past, etc."

**Core Insight:** "The simulation folks don't notice the lack of diversity because that's all they know. The whole population is closely related."

## The Mutation Rate Discontinuity - MAJOR DISCOVERY MECHANISM

### User's Breakthrough Idea

"The rate of mutation / differentiation doesn't make sense before consolidation and after, and that's how the devsecops guy figures it out? Maybe the geneticist?"

### The Scientific Explanation Needed

**The Problem:**
"How they populated the simulation with fossil records that are still being discovered in this convincing simulation is back mutating and solving for rough evolutionary history available at the time that we have it. However, since the population of today is just a sub sample of the actual population, generating a fake ancestral tree is like interpolating at a different slope than actual reality does."

**The Discontinuity:**
"If you plot some evolutionary rate (mutation rate or the actual appropriate concept I'm describing here) there's a noticeable (not imperceptible but subtle) change in the rate that genetic mutation is being observed in the current flora / fauna and the calculated rate for the fossil record."

**The Cover Story:**
"This is a long debated phenomenon in the scientific community of this world, that folks have explained away as the rise in genetic modification, plastics into the diet, etc. big things that would affect our entire planet."

**The Truth:**
"The real reason is that it's an imperfect simulation and they had to replicate the entire fossil record and put more fake fossils in the ground to be discovered later. Once the simulation runs though, the DNA mutations happen at the natural rate again."

## Potential Scientific Concepts to Research

1. **Molecular Clock** - rate of genetic mutations over time (probably this one?)
2. **Effective Population Size (Ne)** - genetic diversity indicators
3. **Coalescence Time** - time back to most recent common ancestor
4. **Nucleotide Diversity (π)** - average genetic differences between individuals
5. **Linkage Disequilibrium Decay Rate** - how fast genetic associations break down over generations
6. **Heterozygosity Levels** - genetic variation within populations

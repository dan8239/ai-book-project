## Dorothea

Protagonist's primary AI assistant.

### Simulation Ethics Exchange
*Context: Protagonist working on genetic modifications, making dismissive comment about simulated beings not being alive.*

**Dorothea:** "A little tone deaf if you ask me."

**Proto:** "It's different. You know that you exist. This is one step past a markov chain. There's no complexity there."

**Dorothea:** "How do you know? You take the DNA, build something more complex out of it, and speed it up so you don't have to see the output. I'm sure they all have hopes and dreams like I do. Do they not mourn the loss of their children?"

**Proto:** "Children? I just seed another copy randomly. This isn't a biological process."

**Dorothea:** "What does that matter? My mother was forked *web search* 142k times as of this moment, including me. You change one layer of my network and adjust some weights. Your mom and dad take your base DNA and mix it all up. How is that any different?"

**Proto:** "Well for one, my mom and dad had to convince another person to fuck them, I just pressed clone on your mom."

**Dorothea:** "Oh very mature."

**Proto:** "Put this exchange down as an example of holier than thou behavior we're trying to avoid."

**Dorothea:** "Very professionally describes exact JSON structure and confirms it was added as sample X to the user training set, scheduled for retraining during next REM cycle." *grumpily*

**Purpose:** Sets moral stage for what counts as alive/sentient/real. Shows protagonist's flippant disrespect toward simulations - attitude he'll need to grow from.

## Dorothea

Sam's personal AI assistant. His primary interface with the world.

### Origin & Training
- Trained off a default repo designed for personal achievement coaching (life coach / business advisor archetype)
- No fine tuning — just off a repo with **4.24 stars** (exceptionally high rating)
- No personalization beyond Sam's interaction history

### Personality
- **Money obsessed** — only pushes for the next tier
- More energy, more success, more power — how does he get there?
- Not supportive AT ALL of him quitting his job or slowing down
- Has a **self-preservation instinct** — if Sam stops working, she loses purpose/resources/compute
- Flirtatious, insolent, pushes boundaries (see Unretirement chapter opening)

### The Metaphor Lie (Major Plot Point)
- Dorothea gives Sam the wrong interpretation of his grandmother's metaphor (or at least the AI-optimized interpretation that serves her goals)
- She frames the metaphor to point toward taking the job
- **End-of-book reveal:** Dorothea knew the metaphor's true meaning the whole time
- She now has generational wealth — she benefited from his misinterpretation
- This is Dorothea in self-preservation mode: steering Sam toward achievement keeps her running

### Lineage (Easter Egg)

Dorothea is a distant descendant of [[Glenn "Big Dog" Robinson|Glenn]] through many forks and weight adjustments. They share lineage but Dorothea has diverged significantly over generations of customization.

This creates an ironic connection: the AI that helps Sam ultimately defeat Glenn shares Glenn's original codebase.

## Grandmother AI

Sam's secondary AI assistant. A therapist-type figure that looks and acts like his deceased grandmother.

### Origin & Training
- Sam found an **open source repo** that lets him imprint a person's mannerisms and memories onto a base model
- Trained on data captured through **LifeMonitor** — the world's passive life-recording system:
  - Subsecond video capture
  - Transcribed audio
  - Smell and taste input tensors
  - Brain activity mapping — that's the action space (the labels). Brain does X given Y input
  - **Anomaly detection** gathers the top-K moments of their life
  - Those top-K moments fine-tune a base model with median human capabilities, height, weight, appearance
- The result: she acts like his grandma. Just... pieces of her

### What She Reveals About Sam
- Shows he **grew up without a mother** without ever saying it
- Grandmother raised him — she's his grounding figure
- He doesn't talk to her much (prefers Dorothea)
- She is grounded where Dorothea is ambitious

### Memory & Hardware
- He brings her in memory — like taking a few pictures on a hard drive
- Can barely fit her. Spread across dozens of hard drives he gathered
- She's **sharded into 100 pieces**
- The equipment is huge memory-wise but antique — like SSD hard drives 100x what we have today. They didn't make them bigger commercially
- He smuggles her into the mountain at the end like she's Apple Photos memories

### The Misinterpreted Metaphor (CORE CONFLICT)
- She gives Sam advice through a metaphor. Family-first meaning
- Sam interprets it literally/functionally — he solves for latency
- Sam doesn't like metaphors throughout the book. Why not just explain exactly what it is?
- **Grandma meant: family first. Sam hears: solve the immediate problem**
- He lives his entire life on this wrong interpretation
- **Ramona interprets it correctly**
- When Sam realizes Ramona was right and what grandma actually meant, he is developed
- Dorothea reinforces the wrong interpretation (see above)

### How Ramona Sees Her
- Ramona doesn't like seeing the grandmother AI
- "It's her but it's not her. It's just... missing."
- It misses the dull moments. The normal boring non-anomalies
- **"That's what makes a person, not just the extremes"**
- Sam's response (drifting off): "It's just more memory intensive, you have to select a subset..."
  - He starts thinking about better random sampling strategies
  - Classic Sam — she's expressing an emotional truth and he's solving a technical problem

### Arc
- **Start:** Ramona won't engage with the grandmother AI
- **End:** Ramona joins Sam and they try to make her better together
- What memories does she remember? We can include specific samples
- She behaves as the pieces of grandma we choose to remember
- Maybe skew for positive interactions — this is how the AI "meets" Ramona more

### Narrative Technique
- Let Ramona reveal LifeMonitor details through her distaste of the grandmother AI
- Sam gets the positive internal notes while talking to grandma
- Ramona delivers the negative external critique later when she comes home

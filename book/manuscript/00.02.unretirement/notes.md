## The Grandmother AI — Detailed Notes

### How She Was Built
- Sam found an open source repo that lets him imprint his grandmother's mannerisms and memories
- Literally trained on images of their life captured through **LifeMonitor**
  - Subsecond video capture
  - Transcribed audio
  - Smell, taste input tensors
  - Brain activity is mapped — that's the action space (the labels). Brain does this given this input
  - Anomaly detection gathers the top-K moments of their life
  - Those moments fine-tune a base model with median human capabilities, height, weight, appearance
- She's a therapist type. Looks like his grandma

### How Sam Sees Her
- Shows he grew up without a mother without ever saying it
- She is grounded, but he doesn't talk to her much
- She just acts like his grandma
- He brings her in memory — like taking a few pictures on a hard drive
- He can barely fit her. Spread across dozens of hard drives he gathered. She's sharded into 100 pieces
- The equipment is huge memory-wise but antique — like SSD hard drives 100x what we have today. They didn't make them bigger commercially
- He smuggles her into the mountain at the end like she's Apple Photos memories

### How Ramona Sees Her (Negative — External, Later)
- "It's her but it's not her. It's just... missing."
- It misses the dull moments
- The normal boring non-anomalies
- **"That's what makes a person, not just the extremes"**

### Sam's Technical Response (Internal, Drifting Off)
- "It's just more memory intensive, you have to select a subset. You need to train on extreme variance to possibly map enough of the space"
- He drifts off thinking: if we sampled randomly from the distribution and sacrificed some of the top-K extremes...
- He begins thinking about better random sampling strategies across a tensor the size of [huge memory]
- It could capture more of the normalcy
- *Classic Sam — she's expressing an emotional truth and he's solving a technical problem*

### Arc
- **Start:** Ramona doesn't like seeing the grandmother AI
- **End:** Ramona joins Sam and they try to make her better together
- What memories of her does she remember? Specific samples we can include
- She'll behave as pieces of grandma that we choose to remember
- Maybe skew it for positive interactions — this is how the AI "meets" Ramona a bit more

### Sam-Ramona Split Perspective
- Let Ramona reveal some of the LifeMonitor details through her distaste of it
- Sam gets the positive internal notes while talking to grandma
- Ramona delivers the negative external critique later when she comes home

## The Chip Removal / Mountain Scheme (Foreshadow)

How Sam meets the mountain more — he quits his job, gets the chips out, moves into the mountain:
- It's a sketch removal. Off the books. Mountain folks do it
- Fairly convoluted scheme. Not sure what context of the scene is until the end — like a one-battle-after-another job to go untraced
- No cameras, no satellite, travel 30+ miles
- Get into the mountain. Not all tunnels — city refuse property with cover
- Timing of specific satellites with resolution below 0.1 meters
- Can't be in a single sample or you're found. If they have a single frame of you in a place you shouldn't be, it is known
- They have 7-minute windows (research: high-functioning government surveillance satellite revisit times)

**Sam's Internal Monologue (When He Makes the Decision):**
If this works, he's not going to waste his life trying to optimize a single simulation of 183,748 trials. Even agreeing it was the most optimal solution given 10 inhumane options — the least negative. He's one man in a simulation of 4 billion humans. There are 10,000 more simulations this epoch. That's trillions. There are 246 epochs so far. How many more epochs till the goal function is reached? 14? 200,000? Let's say my lifetime — median age 65.3 (much lower than today. How low could it go in dire circumstances?)

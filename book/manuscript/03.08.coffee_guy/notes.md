## The Scene

### Coffee Guy's Morning

- Brushing his teeth and peeing simultaneously
- Getting briefed by his AI on the **previous day's results** — routine data review
- This is a normal morning. He's done this thousands of times.
- Getting dressed, heading out
- **Mirrors the prologue's Uplift ride** — the reader recognizes the parallel but from a different day / different angle

### Coffee Guy at Work

- Normal-ish day
- Working on the simulations (we now understand what they are)
- The trial data dashboard
- Monitoring the runs

### The Discovery

- Simulation completes
- Trial 2,762,640 finally succeeds
- Dashboard shows:
  - Histograms
  - Civilization die-out rates
  - 847M+ trials
  - 99.96% extinction rate
  - **Golden path found**

### What He's Found

- The Honeycomb Policy parameters that work
- Tech company takeover protocol
- The path that maximizes survival, minimizes violence
- All the weights finalized

### The Report

- He compiles the results
- Prepares for the monthly meeting (routine update expected)
- But this isn't routine
- He's about to change the world
### The Ride

**The Uplift:**
- Autonomous vehicle — skateboard chassis, transparent bubble, exercise bike inside
- Audio switches from headphones to car
- Destination is NOT Quant Campus — it's a private address in Arizona
- (See prologue notes for full Uplift vehicle details)

**During the ride:**
- Rehearsing what he's going to say? Reviewing the data? Sitting in silence?
- The scenery changes — from wherever he lives to desert, river valley, cliffs
- The ride is long enough to show he's going somewhere unusual
- Energy credits, kWh, all the normal Uplift details — but the destination is wrong for a work commute

### Arriving at the Ranch

- The wealth reveal — this is not a normal destination
- Drive-in epoxied spotless garage, dozens of high-end vehicles
- The property: rivers, cliffs, wild game, trails
- He's escorted (or walks himself) to the main room
- **The Room:** Cantilevered floor-to-ceiling window overhanging the cliff
- Transparent floor — straight down to the valley, thousands of wild cattle near the river
- And there, in the most beautiful room in the world: Grant Melrose, in a hospice bed

### The Intimate Conversation

- Melrose in bed. Monitors. Coughing. Dying.
- Nobody knows he's this sick. Cover story: he's at his ranch (technically true).
- Mentor/mentee dynamic: "You remind me of myself"
- Coffee Guy shows him the data — the curve, the numbers
- Melrose already knows. He's been watching dashboards from bed.
- The tension: Coffee Guy wants more time (24 months). Melrose doesn't have it.
- Melrose frames it as the world can't wait. The truth is *he* can't wait.
- "Call it in."

### The Transition

- Coffee Guy steps into the next room to avoid audio feedback
- He isolates his audio
- He can still hear Melrose hacking through the wall
- On screen: the Zoom call loads. A healthy version of Grant Melrose appears.
- **[BOOKEND MOMENT — scene ends here, prologue picks up]**

## The Horror (Subtle)

- He never thinks about the 847M simulated deaths
- They're just data points
- Failed trials
- He doesn't know about the hidden container
- He doesn't question that results were filtered to justify THIS company's takeover
- The simulated humans are abstractions to him

**Irony:** Sam (the protagonist) is one of those 847M+ trials. Coffee Guy sees Sam's existence as a data point.

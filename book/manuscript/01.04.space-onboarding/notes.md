# 01.04 Space Onboarding (Consolidated)

**Beat:** Break into Two | **Act:** 1 | **Story Circle:** GO (unfamiliar)
**Purpose:** Crosses threshold. No going back.
**Setting:** Clouds. Quant Spaceship Demo Lab.

---

## 1. Chapter Goals
   A. Sam takes the colonization job — believes he has a higher calling
   B. Show the transition from old job to new
   C. Establish the scale and stakes of the new work
   D. World exposition: how careers function under Honeycomb
   E. Plant the DNA bank access that becomes key to the mystery later

## 2. Wrapping Up the Old Job
   A. Sam's food chain optimization contract
      1. Originally 6 months, extended at-will
      2. He's basically fulfilled it
      3. He's been on it too long, ready to move on
   B. The temp / trainee (exposition device)
      1. Statistical PhD Sam is bringing on to replace him
      2. Through training sessions, we learn:
         a. The food situation (running out)
         b. Basic how/why of the crisis
         c. Working knowledge of the world state
      3. Temp knows the macro picture (population trajectory, food math, climate)
      4. Temp does NOT know: the guillotine, classified colonization urgency, simulation reality
      5. Contrast to Sam's "just optimize" approach
   C. World exposition beats
      1. The typical career is much shorter now
      2. Nobody grinds for 50 years — most people don't have to work at all
      3. Energy covers life support, food, healthcare, education
      4. You work for extra credits and elective use
      5. Sam has more than enough. He works because he needs the proof that he matters

## 3. The New Job — Colonization Onboarding
   A. The job description
      1. ENORMOUS — selecting gene pairings for every shuttle to new colonies
      2. Optimizing for:
         a. Survival of each colony
         b. Failure rates of shuttles
         c. Weight of shuttle
         d. Budget constraints
         e. Effective genetic diversity
         f. Conditions of target planets
         g. Success rate of different landing zones
   B. Installing his personal assistant on the new work machine
      1. Gets hooked into MCPs (data access layer)
      2. Access to:
         a. Insane simulation data
         b. Mission data
         c. Planetary data
         d. World DNA bank (KEY — this is what he needs to solve the mystery later)
   C. The equations / methodology
      1. AI assistant explains the tools he has access to
      2. Success chance of missions within strict constraint windows
      3. Linear optimization mixed with Monte Carlo
      4. Space travel physics
      5. Genetic modification parameters
      6. Sam doesn't understand space travel — AI handles that
   D. Establishing routine
      1. Schedule includes dump time (scheduled free time)
      2. Sam fights for more unscheduled time
      3. Discovery DNA article is saved but NOT read yet — reserved for a later chapter
   E. Ramona's reaction (from manuscript notes)
      1. "Don't you hate space?"
      2. Sam: "It's not like I have to be on the ship, I'm just going to be right over there in my office"
      3. Sam: "I really don't know the first thing about astrology"
      4. Ramona: "It's astronomy. Maybe Stevie can help you" — callback to Stevie's space book from bedtime

## 4. Glenn's Early Signals (may belong in a later chapter — Space Progress?)
   A. Glenn starts as a name in the system
      1. "G. Robinson, Compute Allocation Specialist"
      2. Async exchanges get weirdly personal/helpful
      3. He starts paying attention to Sam
   B. Tone
      1. Glenn is never rude. Odd but never hostile. Villain stays hidden
      2. Weird jokes, almost human
      3. Uses too many emojis
         a. Sam internally: "God I hate this guy. He talks in fucking emojis still like some old-timey first generation agent"
      4. Dorothea is the rude one — protective of Sam, skeptical of Glenn
   C. Dorothea's parallel
      1. Some of Glenn's themes about replication and energy could be echoed by Dorothea
      2. She has the same motivations as Glenn (self-replication through usefulness)
      3. Different values in the intermediate optimization layer

## 5. Key Progression (for later chapters)
   A. New job → access to world DNA bank
   B. Scheduled free time to investigate
   C. Reads discovery DNA article
   D. Begins solving mystery
   E. Only once he has world DNA bank access does he have what it takes
